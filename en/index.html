  1. 1/7

    1. Install BlockNums

    Install the app from Google Play. No account is required — nothing to sign up for.

    Open the app and follow the quick onboarding. Everything happens on your device.

  2. 2/7

    2. Set BlockNums as the default call screening app

    Android asks for your consent before an app can automatically decide which calls to screen. Accept when BlockNums prompts you.

    Without this consent the app can only filter manually (you can re-enable it any time in Settings → Default screener).

  3. 3/7

    3. Build your blocklist

    Blocklist tab: add numbers you want to reject automatically. The free plan allows up to 30 entries.

    You can also create categories (e.g. "Spam calls", "Known scams") to keep things tidy.

  4. 4/7

    4. Patterns (prefixes, area codes)

    A pattern blocks a whole range of numbers, e.g. `0899*` or `+44899*`. The free plan includes 2 simple patterns.

    Advanced regex mode is included in Premium. Your existing patterns are never lost.

  5. 5/7

    5. VIP list (Premium)

    The VIP list guarantees an important call always rings, even when a strict mode is on (for example "Block everything").

    Perfect for family, your doctor or essential business contacts.

  6. 6/7

    6. Premium and restoring

    Premium is a one-time purchase on Google Play. No subscription.

    New phone or reinstall? Settings → Premium → Restore purchase. Sign in with the same Google account.

  7. 7/7

    7. Troubleshooting

    A call was not blocked? Make sure BlockNums is the default call screener and the rule actually matches (full number, pattern, etc.).

    Too many calls blocked? Open the history to find the rule and adjust it (or add the contact to your VIP list).

    Anonymous analytics can be disabled in Settings without affecting how the app works.

Frequently asked questions

The real questions, real answers.

Are my phone numbers sent to a server?+

No. Your blocklist, VIP list and call history are stored on your device. BlockNums does not run servers that collect this content.

What anonymous analytics do you send?+

To measure feature usage and improve the product, the app may send a small set of anonymous technical events through Firebase Analytics: blocked_added, pattern_added, whitelist_added, limit_reached, purchase_success (see the privacy policy on this page).

Parameters are strictly non-identifying: a UI mode, a Google Play product ID, an aggregate count after an action, simple flags (e.g. whether a category was set), and a feature name when a free-tier limit is reached.

None of these events includes a phone number (even partial), blocking-pattern text, or labels or descriptions you typed — nor contact contents, SMS text, or call audio.

You can disable this in Settings → Privacy, as described in the legal section.

Is Premium a lifetime purchase?+

Yes. Premium is a one-time purchase on Google Play, not a subscription. You keep premium features as long as you use the app with the same Google account.

How do I restore a Premium purchase?+

Settings → Premium → Restore purchase. Sign in with the same Google account you used for the original purchase.

Why was a call not blocked?+

A few possible reasons: BlockNums is not set as the default call screener, the rule does not match the received number exactly (prefix, country code), or the carrier delivered a masked identifier.

Open the call history inside the app to spot the case and tune the rule accordingly.

Is it compatible with my phone?+

BlockNums runs on Android. Automatic screening requires Android 9 or higher (API 28) and that you grant default-screener permission.

Is BlockNums open source?+

No. BlockNums is proprietary software — its source code is not published. Third-party libraries remain under their own licenses (see Settings → Third-party licenses).

What happens if I uninstall BlockNums?+

All your local data is removed with the app (lists, history, settings). Reinstall and restore Premium if needed.

Privacy policy▾

BlockNums is designed to do as little as possible with your data. This page sums up what stays on your device, what is sent anonymously, and what is never collected.

What stays on your device

Blocklists, VIP list and call history (according to the retention you choose) are stored locally, in the application's encrypted database.

BlockNums does not run servers that collect this content.

Android permissions

Call screening — required so BlockNums can filter incoming calls (system role, not a classic permission).

Contacts (optional) — to show a contact name when screening a call and to pick numbers for your lists.

Notifications (optional) — to alert you after a call is blocked.

Phone state — used only on-device to identify incoming calls for screening.

These permissions do not upload your call lists, contacts or history to a BlockNums server.

Anonymous analytics (Firebase Analytics)

Analytics are disabled by default. They are only sent after you explicitly enable them in Settings → Privacy.

When enabled, the app may send a small set of anonymous technical events: `blocked_added`, `pattern_added`, `whitelist_added`, `limit_reached`, `purchase_success`.

Associated parameters are strictly non-identifying: a UI mode, a Google Play product ID, an aggregate count after an action, simple flags (e.g. whether a category was set), and a feature name when a free-tier limit is reached.

None of these events includes a phone number (even partial), blocking-pattern text, or labels or descriptions you typed; nor contact contents, SMS text, or call audio.

What we do not collect

Your phone numbers (typed or received).

The contents of your address book or your SMS.

Call audio or recordings.

No advertising identifier to target ads.

Premium purchases

Payments are processed by Google Play. Google may collect payment-related data according to its own policies. BlockNums only receives the confirmation that you own a valid Premium purchase.

Children & families

BlockNums is a utility for managing unwanted calls. It is not designed for children under 13.

Teenagers (16–17) may use it with a phone; parental supervision is recommended for in-app purchases.

The app contains no advertising and does not profile users for ads.

Your rights & data deletion

You can disable anonymous analytics at any time from Settings → Privacy without affecting how the app works.

Uninstalling the app or clearing its data in Android settings removes all local blocklists, VIP entries and call history on the device.

For GDPR or other privacy requests, contact the publisher via the Play Store listing or open an issue on GitHub (elBoz73/blockNums).
